Early Life

Birth

Burroughs was born in 1914 and he was a son of a wealthy family. He spent his early years in his hometown St.Louis, Missouri.He was the grandson of of the inventor and founder of the Burroughs Corporation, William Seward Burroughs I, and nephew of public relations manager Ivy Lee.

Education

He attended John Burroughs School in St. Louis.Then he went to Los Alamos boarding school, where he found out that he was gay.He studied English Literature in Harvard.While he was there, Burroughs made trips to New York City and was introduced to the gay subculture.

First Steps

Burroughs graduated from Harvard in 1936. His parents had decided to give him a monthly allowance of $200.It was enough to keep him going, and it guaranteed his survival for the next twenty-five years. The allowance was a ticket to freedom; it allowed him to live where he wanted to and to forgo employment.

Adult Life

Army

Burroughs enlisted in the US army in 1942 after the bombing of Pearl Harbor. After that he was diagnosed with depression and he his mother got him a civilian disability discharge due to previous mental instability.Burroughs and Kerouac got into trouble with the law for failing to report a murder involving Lucien Carr. They wrote a novel about this incident titled "And the Hippos Were Boiled in Their Tanks".

Vollmer's Death

Then he moved in Chicago where he lived with his fiance Joan Vollmer, Jack Kerouak and his wife. They became addicted in various drugs and relocated to Mexico city because Burroughs wanted to escape from possible detention to Lousiana's state prison. In Mexico Burrough's accidentally shot Vollmer on the head during a game.

Literary Career

Vollmer's Death was an important event in his life, and the one that made him persue a writer's career. He became famous under the pen name William Lee when he published Junkie: Confessions of an Unredeemed Drug Addict in 1953. His work includes some excellent piece of Beat Generation writings like Naked Lunch and Queer.

Later Years

Overseas

During 1953 Burroughs spend time with his parents in Florida because he was facing legal problems. Then, he went to Rome to meet Alan Ansen on a vacation financed from his parents' continuing support. He then headed to Tangier, Morocco a place where drugs were freely. He spent the next four years there working on the fiction that would later become Naked Lunch under the strong influence of marijuana.

Kansas

Burroughs moved to Lawrence, Kansas in 1981, taking up residence at 1927 Learnard Avenue where he would spend the rest of his life. He once told a reporter that he was content to live in Kansas, saying, "The thing I like about Kansas is that it's not nearly as violent, and it's a helluva lot cheaper. He then became a counterculture icon which inspired 1970s proto-punk rock band Doctors of Madness.

Death

Burroughs died August 2, 1997 in Lawrence, Kansas from complications o f a heart attack he had suffered the previous day. He was interred in the family plot in Bellefontaine Cemetery in St. Louis, Missouri, with a marker bearing his full name and the epitaph "American Writer".

Famous Writings

Junkie

Junkie: Confessions of an Unredeemed Drug Addict is a semi-autobiographical novel. and focuses on Burroughs' life as a drug user and dealer. It is considered to be one of the most important novels that show the life of a drug addict.

Naked Lunch

Naked Lunch is a Novel which was published in 1959. The book is a series of loosely connected stories which can be read in any order. It follows the narration of the Junkie's main character who travels around the world to find drugs. It is considered on of the top 100 books of American literature according to Time magazine.

Queer

Queer is a sequel of the Junkie novel written between 1951 and 1953. The main character goes to south America to find a substance of a drug. It had not been published since 1985 because Burroughs found it traumatic due to the fact that it was written while he was waiting for the trial of the accidental homicide of his wife.
